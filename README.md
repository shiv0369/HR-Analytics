# HR Analytics Capstone Project
### Comprehensive Analytics Solution: Raw Data → SQL Processing → Statistical Validation → Business Intelligence

Transforming unstructured HR datasets into actionable business intelligence by building a complete end-to-end analytics pipeline. This project identifies employee turnover trends, pay equity gaps, recruitment effectiveness, and workforce optimization opportunities using SQL, Python, and Power BI.

---

# Executive Summary

This capstone project demonstrates the complete data analytics lifecycle—from data engineering and ETL to statistical analysis and executive-level business intelligence reporting.

The solution processes multi-format HR datasets and delivers actionable insights for HR leaders to improve employee retention, optimize recruitment spending, and support data-driven workforce planning.

## Key Highlights

- Processed **301 employee records**
- Integrated data across **6 departments**
- Analyzed **22 recruitment channels**
- Covered **11 years of historical HR data**
- Identified **32.6% employee turnover rate**
- Estimated **$6.28M annual productivity loss** due to employee turnover
- Built interactive executive dashboards using Power BI
- Validated analytical results across SQL, Python, and Power BI

---

# Business Problem

Organizations often collect HR data from multiple disconnected sources but struggle to transform it into meaningful business insights.

This project answers critical business questions such as:

- Which departments experience the highest turnover?
- How much does employee turnover cost the organization?
- Are employees being paid fairly?
- Which recruitment channels provide the best return on investment?
- Which employees are at the highest risk of leaving?

# Key Business Insights

## Employee Turnover

- Employee turnover rate: **32.6%**
- Production department recorded the highest turnover.
- Estimated annual productivity loss:

> **~$6.28 Million**

*(98 employees × $64.1K average salary)*

---

## Pay Equity

- Average gender pay gap:

> **$8,320**

- Female compensation averages approximately **88%** of male compensation.

---

## Recruitment ROI

Recruitment channel analysis identified significant opportunities for cost optimization.

Potential annual savings:

> **$50,000+**

by reallocating hiring budgets toward higher-performing recruitment channels.

---

## Employee Retention Risk

Analysis identified:

- **230 employees**
- **76.9% of workforce**

as potential flight risks due to below-market compensation.

---

# Business Recommendations

## Improve Employee Retention

Focus retention initiatives on high-turnover departments, particularly Production.

**Estimated Annual Savings**

> ~$1.2 Million

---

## Review Compensation Strategy

- Address pay equity gaps
- Benchmark salaries
- Improve employee satisfaction
- Reduce compliance risk

---

## Optimize Recruitment Spending

Reduce spending on expensive recruitment platforms.

Example:

- CareerBuilder
  - ~$7.8K per hire

Increase investment in:

- Employee referral programs
- High-performing recruitment channels

---

## Strengthen Leadership

Improve management practices in departments with disproportionately high employee turnover.
